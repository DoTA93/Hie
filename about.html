Our Story

HIE (Haddington International Education) was founded when Noreen Lucey identified an opportunity to enhance university collaboration and student recruitment efforts by creating a local presence on behalf of universities in various regions globally.

From working in the International Offices of colleges and universities, Noreen saw the need to provide support to universities that wanted to expand their network globally but did not have the time or resources to do so. Her time spent living overseas in Europe, Australia, China and Central America, reinforced the value of working on the ground to effectively manage a local network.

Over the years, Noreen has been joined by like-minded people who understand the importance of international education in the region they live in, and HIE is now made up of consultants based in Europe, Middle East, and Asia.

As a result, HIE has been able to develop and manage strong partnerships on behalf of universities and agencies across much of the globe and continues to evolve with ever-changing markets and student needs.

Our goal is to improve the internationalization of campuses globally and the overall study experience of students overseas. We aim to approach international education holistically by working with students, academia, industry and social enterprises in the countries we are present in.

Out Story

Noreen Lucey - Founder

Noreen has worked and lived abroad for much of her career and holds degrees in European Studies, Languages and International Relations.

She has worked in the computer software, insurance, finance and real estate industry and accidentally stumbled across a career in International Education whilst living in Central America. She moved from Nicaragua to China where she spent several years developing a local network of agents and universities in East Asia.

Now based in Europe, she enjoys working with European universities in creating opportunities for their students to study overseas. She currently facilitates partnership development for international institutions seeking to expand their footprint in Europe also.

Her real passion, and that of the people she works with, is in sharing their international education experience to be more inclusive and enable more students to experience study abroad. Her team is constantly seeking to broaden student and faculty experiences overseas.

Her dream career has led her to work with schools, colleges, universities and agents in much of the world and she finds inspiration in the people she works with and can’t imagine a more rewarding work.

Linda Huskes - Director of Training & Development

Linda has worked for various educational institutions since 2000 and has been active in Higher Education since 2006. She holds an MA and an M.Ed. and has been teaching in the field of English and Intercultural Skills for over 15 years.

She held the role of Head of International Office at Fonty’s International Business School, The Netherlands.

She brought her vast network and knowledge of the market to establish a Training & Development program. This is to ensure that students and faculty are prepared for the cultural challenges that study overseas can bring.
